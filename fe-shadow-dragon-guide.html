Recruitment & Special Conditions

Gaiden Chapter Unlock Conditions

Gaiden chapters unlock when your army has 15 or fewer living units at the end of the preceding chapter. Red-bordered cards below indicate characters tied to Gaiden chapters. Planning which units to sacrifice — ideally D-tier units you recruited just for this — is a key part of Shadow Dragon strategy.
Navarre
Chapter 3 · Enemy unit on the map
Starts as an enemy. Have Caeda talk to him during the chapter. Do not attack or kill him. He joins immediately after the conversation.
One of the best units — never miss this recruitment.
Lena & Julian
Chapter 3 · NPCs
Both join automatically — no special condition. Lena is a Cleric NPC on the map; Julian is a Thief escorting her. Move a unit to their tile or reach the area to trigger them joining.
Julian is the only thief in the game. Keep him alive for chest access.
Castor
Chapter 3 · Enemy unit
Starts as an enemy Hunter. Have Caeda talk to him to recruit. Mediocre unit — recruit to pad your roster for Gaiden threshold, then consider sacrificing.
Dolph & Macellan
Chapters 4–5 · Enemy Armor Knights
Both are enemy units. Have Caeda talk to each individually. Recruit as sacrifice candidates for the Gaiden system.
Roger
Chapter 5 · Enemy Armor Knight
Has a crush on Caeda — have Caeda talk to him. Weak armor unit, mostly useful for the roster body count toward Gaiden chapters.
Matthis
Chapter 5 · Enemy Cavalier
Lena's brother. Have Lena talk to him (Caeda also works). Mediocre cavalier — sacrifice candidate.
Wendell
Gaiden 7x · Auto-recruit
Gaiden 7x unlocks after Chapter 7 if you have 15 or fewer units alive. Wendell joins automatically at the start of the Gaiden map — no action needed. Already promoted Sage.
Worth engineering a sacrifice to unlock — he's your only promoted magic user before Linde.
Merric
Chapter 5 · Joins automatically
Joins at the start of Chapter 5. No special condition required.
Hardin
Chapter 8 · Joins automatically
Joins at the start of Chapter 8 with his group. No special condition. Prioritize him immediately — he's one of the best units in the game.
Maria
Chapter 10 · NPC prisoner
Maria is an NPC on the map. Move any unit to her tile to rescue her and add her to your party. You need her (or Palla) to recruit Minerva.
Minerva
Chapter 10 · Enemy commander (or Ch. 14 auto)
Minerva leads the enemy Whitewings. Have Palla or Maria talk to her during Ch. 10. If not recruited here, she joins automatically as an ally at the start of Chapter 14. Always recruit in Ch. 10 — you don't want to go 4 chapters without her.
Rescue Maria first on the map, then use Maria or Palla to approach Minerva.
Palla & Catria
Chapter 10 (or Ch. 14) · Auto-join with Minerva
They are part of Minerva's Whitewing group and join your side automatically the moment Minerva is recruited. No separate action needed for them.
Linde
Chapter 11 · Joins automatically
Joins at the start of Chapter 11. No special condition. Invest in her immediately — she's the best magic unit in the game.
Jeorge
Chapter 14 · Enemy unit
Appears as an enemy Sniper. Have any unit talk to him (or Marth) to recruit — no special character requirement. Reach him before he's killed by other enemies.
Lorenz
Chapter 14 · Enemy General
Enemy General. Have Caeda talk to him to recruit. Weak old unit — recruit as a sacrifice candidate for later Gaiden chapters if needed.
Midia & others
Gaiden 16x · NPC rescues
Gaiden 16x unlocks after Chapter 15 with 15 or fewer units alive. Midia and several other units appear as NPCs you rescue by moving to their locations. No talk required — just reach them.
Midia is a decent already-promoted Paladin, but most others from this chapter are weaker.
Xane
Gaiden 19x · Joins automatically
Gaiden 19x unlocks after Chapter 18 with 15 or fewer units alive. Xane joins automatically on the map. His presence in your army is what makes Gaiden 19x accessible — the game checks if you have him.
Critical: You must have Xane to unlock 19x, and you must complete 19x to get Tiki.
Tiki
Gaiden 19x · Bantu must talk to her
Tiki starts as a dangerous boss-level awakened dragon in Gaiden 19x. Bantu must be alive in your party — move Bantu adjacent to Tiki and have him talk to her. She calms down and joins your party.
Requirements chain: sacrifice to unlock 19x → have Xane (enables 19x) → keep Bantu alive → Bantu talks to Tiki. Plan your sacrifices carefully around keeping Bantu.

Reclassification Recommendations

Shadow Dragon's reclassing system lets most units change class. When you reclass, your level resets to 1 in the new class but carries over your accumulated stats — making units with strong base stats or high-growth units especially powerful when reclassed. The main benefits are new weapon types, movement type changes (especially gaining flying or mounted), and class skill bonuses like Swordmaster's +15 crit. Use Master Seals to promote; unpromoted units can reclass freely.
Ogma
Default: Mercenary → Hero
  • Swordmaster — +15 crit bonus, lethal with Killing Edge builds
  • Hero — Sword + Axe versatility, more bulk than Swordmaster
  • Sniper — Gives 2-range coverage if your team lacks bows
Recommended: Swordmaster for crit-focused play, Hero for all-around versatility. Both are strong — choose based on team needs.
Navarre
Default: Myrmidon → Swordmaster
  • Swordmaster — Keep for maximum crit rate; pairs with Killing Edge
  • Hero — Fixes Str weakness with Axe access and more bulk
If Str is lagging in the mid-game, reclass to Hero early. If he's hitting consistently, stay Swordmaster for the crit bonus — it's one of the best in the game.
Caeda
Default: Pegasus Knight → Falcon Knight
  • Falcon Knight — Keeps Wing Spear usable + high speed + sword access
  • Dracoknight — Lance + Axe, more Def and Str, loses sword weakness
Falcon Knight is recommended — Wing Spear's effectiveness vs armors/cavalry is too valuable to lose. Only reclass if you have another flier covering her role.
Cain
Default: Cavalier → Paladin
  • Dracoknight — Flying mobility on an already well-rounded unit; top pick
  • Paladin — Default; solid mounted utility with sword + lance
  • Hero — Best raw combat if you already have enough fliers/cavalry
Dracoknight is the top recommendation — flying movement on Cain's stats is exceptional for the mid-game. Best of both worlds if you're not already overloaded on fliers.
Gordin
Default: Archer → Sniper
  • Horseman — Mounted + Bow + Sword; massive mobility upgrade (top pick)
  • Sniper — Better accuracy and damage at range, stays foot
Horseman is transformative. It solves Gordin's core weakness (positioning) and gives him sword fallback. Strongly recommended if you're investing in him at all.
Draug
Default: Armor Knight → General
  • Paladin — Trades some bulk for drastically improved movement
  • General — Maximum defense wall for chokepoint holding
Reclass to Paladin if using him — his movement as an Armor/General is his biggest liability. As a Paladin he still has great bulk and can actually keep up with the team.
Merric
Default: Mage → Sage
  • Sage — Balanced magic + staff access; Excalibur stays usable
  • Bishop — Higher staff rank, divine magic; better support role
Stay Sage if you want him to fight. Reclass Bishop if you want a dedicated healer/staffer after Linde arrives and takes over the offensive magic role.
Linde
Default: Mage → Sage
  • Sage — Keep; Aura tome is irreplaceable offensively (recommended)
  • Bishop — Only consider if you desperately need a high-rank staffer
Keep Linde as Sage. Her Aura tome and offensive Magic/Speed growths make her the best combat mage — don't sacrifice that for staff utility.
Hardin
Default: Cavalier → Paladin
  • Paladin — Default; excellent mounted bruiser with solid weapon access
  • General — Near-unkillable wall given his HP/Def growths
  • Dracoknight — Flying mobility on one of the bulkiest units in the game
Hardin excels in any class. Paladin is the safest default. Dracoknight if you want a flying bruiser; General if you want a near-immortal wall. All three are legitimate.
Jeorge
Default: Sniper (already promoted)
  • Horseman — Mounted movement + Bow + Sword; strong upgrade
  • Sniper — Keep for Parthia's +5 Res and raw bow damage
Horseman adds mobility but you lose Parthia's Res bonus in that class. If your team already has magic coverage, go Horseman. If you need the Res tanking, stay Sniper.
Palla / Catria
Default: Pegasus Knight → Falcon Knight
  • Dracoknight — More Str and Def, Lance + Axe coverage
  • Falcon Knight — Keep for speed and sword access
If running Caeda and Minerva already, reclass one to Dracoknight for weapon variety. Catria benefits more from Dracoknight than Palla due to her better speed sustaining the speed loss.
Julian
Default: Thief
  • Assassin — Keeps Lockpick, gains Lethality skill
  • Hero — Good combat stats; loses thief utility
Reclass to Assassin — it keeps his lockpick utility and adds combat viability. Hero is an option if you have all chests covered and want pure combat.
